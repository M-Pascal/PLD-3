Climate change: refers to long-term shifts in temperatures and weather patterns.
Carbon footprint: this is the amount of carbon dioxide released into the atmosphere.
Global warming: Refer to the increase in temperature of the earth's atmosphere that is caused by the increase of particular gases, especially carbon dioxide.
Biodiversity loss: Refers to the decline or reduction in the variety and abundance of plant and animal species in a specific ecosystem.
Greenhouse gas: Greenhouse gases are gases in Earth's atmosphere that trap heat.
Renewable energy: Refers to energy derived from natural sources that are replenished at a higher rate than they are consumed.
Flooding: Refers to an overflow of water onto normally dry land.
Sustainability: Refers to the ability to be maintained at a certain rate or level.
Sea level rise: Refers to an increase in the level of the world's oceans due to the effects of global warming.
Afforestation: Is the establishment of a forest or stand of trees (forestation) in an area where there was no recent tree cover.
Earth: Earth refers to our home planet.
